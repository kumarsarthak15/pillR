When you walk into a neighbourhood medical shop in Pune, you probably don't think about whether they're licensed. You assume they are. That assumption is usually correct — but it's worth knowing what the licence actually means, especially when you start ordering medicines online.

## What CDSCO actually is

CDSCO stands for the **Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation**. It's the national regulator that decides which medicines can be sold in India and who's allowed to sell them.

Every pharmacy that legally dispenses medicines in India holds a **drug licence**, issued under the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940. The licence specifies:

- Who can dispense (a registered pharmacist must be on-site)
- What categories of medicines are allowed (general, Schedule H, Schedule X, etc.)
- The exact physical location where the pharmacy operates

You can verify any pharmacy's licence number through the State Drugs Control authority. In Maharashtra, that's the FDA Maharashtra portal.

## Why this matters more online than offline

In a physical store, the licence is usually on the wall. You can see the pharmacist. You can read the label on the bottle and check the expiry date.

Online, all of that is invisible.

A 2024 industry estimate suggested that **roughly 20% of medicines sold through unregulated online channels in India are counterfeit, expired, or improperly stored**. That number is contested — the regulators say it's lower, some independent studies say it's higher — but the underlying problem is real. When you can't see the source, you can't verify the safety.

## What you can do as a customer

Whether you order from MediGrab or anywhere else, here's a short checklist for ordering medicines online safely:

1. **Insist on knowing which pharmacy is dispensing the medicine.** "Sold by [Platform Name]" without a pharmacy name is a yellow flag.
2. **Look for the drug licence number on the invoice.** Real pharmacies print it. Counterfeit operations don't.
3. **Check the expiry date when the medicine arrives.** Don't assume — actually read the strip.
4. **Watch the packaging.** Genuine pharmaceutical packaging in India has clear batch numbers, manufacturer details, and MRP printed (not stickered over).
5. **Prefer COD for first orders** when trying a new service. It gives you a chance to inspect what arrived before paying.
